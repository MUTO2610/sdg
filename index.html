Way to Sustainable Living

How does this Work?

This Model works on the concept of Electricty generation by 3 common resources available to us- Wind, Water and Solar(Sunlight). The windmill generate the most amount of energy, but it is the least efficient. The solar panels are the most efficient and produce the most energy. The water turbines are the least efficient and produce the least amount of energy. The idea is to use these resources for generation of energy sustainably without harming the environment. All the energy generated is transmitted to the central power grid which stores the energy and according to need forwards it to the transmission tower which carries it forward to the city and usage areas. The extra energy generated is stored in the batteries which is connected to the central power grid.

Wind Resource

Wind turbines work on a simple principle: instead of using electricity to make wind—like a fan—wind turbines use wind to make electricity. Wind turns the propeller-like blades of a turbine around a rotor, which spins a generator, which creates electricity.

Advantages-

Clean fuel source Zero carbon emissions Use only a fraction of land Don't depend on the combustion of fossil fuels Don't pollute the air like a power plant Wind energy is free

Disadvantages-

It is not a continual energy source. It produces noise pollution. It also produces visual pollution. Birds have been killed by flying into rotational turbine blades. The travel and maintenance cost of turbines increases. It is time-consuming.

Hydropower

A conventional dam holds water in a man-made lake, or reservoir, behind it. When water is released through the dam, it spins a turbine connected to a generator that produces electricity.

Advantages-

Hydropower is a renewable source of energy. Hydropower is fueled by water, making it a clean source of energy. Hydroelectric power is a domestic source of energy, allowing each state to produce its own energy without being reliant on international fuel sources. Impoundment hydropower creates reservoirs that offer recreational opportunities such as fishing, swimming, and boating

Disadvantages-

Environmental consequences The construction of hydroelectric power plants requires the creation of large dams, which can alter the natural flow of the river or stream water, leading to serious environmental consequences. High capital costs Dependence on specific conditions Difficulty in maintenance Geographical constraints

Solar Resource

When the sun shines onto a solar panel, energy from the sunlight is absorbed by the PV cells in the panel. This energy creates electrical charges that move in response to an internal electrical field in the cell, causing electricity to flow.

Advantages-

Energy savings Increased home value Environmental benefits (lower carbon footprint, reduced air pollution) Long-term savings Renewable energy source Tax incentives Low maintenance and upkeep costs Energy independence (when paired with solar battery systems)

Disadvantages-

High upfront cost Environmental impact during manufacturing Limited recycling programs Impact on surrounding area during installation Intermittent energy source Potential increase in property taxes and homeowners insurance Dependence on sunlight Specific roof requirements Storage costs.
